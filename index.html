Hi, Welcome!

My name is Jiamin (佳旻) Xie (谢).

I am a second-year master's student concentrated in Human Language Technology at Johns Hopkins University. My primary research interest is speaker diarization (the problem of "who speaks at when"), speaker recognition, and speech recognition. I am currently working on diarization of children's speech, advised by Leibny Paola Garcia, Daniel Povey, and Sanjeev Khudanpur at Center for Language and Speech Processing (CLSP). Aside from research, I enjoy cooking (a receipe I made long time ago), swimming, and reading.

Projects

Speaker Type Detection for Diarization 2018-Present

I am exploring Bi-LSTM, Conv-LSTM, and Attention nerual architectures in Pytorch to perform speaker type (Male, Female, Child, and etc.) detection. This coarse classification of speakers aims to improve audio segmentation for speaker verification in diarization. [code]

Cognitive Radio Algorithm for UTC Aerospace Systems 2017-2018

Future airplanes depoly wirless sensor networks (WAIC) for its convenience. But the operation band of WAIC is occupied by the Radio Altimeter onboard. In this project, we accomplished a Frequency-Hopping algorithm to detect and mitigate radio interference using MATLAB. [poster]

Signal Analysis for 5G Carrier Waveform (CE-OFDM) 2017-2018

The current LTE downlink scheme OFDM suffers from high peak to average power ratio (PAPR). I investigated a constant envelope variation of the OFDM waveform. I analyzed both systems with different symbol mappings and compared performances in Bit Error Rate. [report]

Underwater Acoustic Signal Processing for AUV 2017-2018

I designed an electric system that handled analog amplification, digitization, and processing (beamforming) of acoustic signals captured by piezoelectric sensors. I created circuit schematics in Eagle to realize this design and fabricated a 4-layer mixed-signal board. [report]
